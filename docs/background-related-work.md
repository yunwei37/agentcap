## Novelty Verdict
- Overall same-claim risk: high. I would not claim the broad idea is unquestionably novel. After the 73-PDF sweep, AuthGraph, PACT, AIRGuard, ARGUS, Agent-Sentry, ClawGuard, VIGIL, and related IFC/policy systems directly occupy much of the intent/provenance/authority-control/runtime-guard language. The safe claim is narrower: IntentCap is a run-centric extension authorization compiler/checker that packages context influence over authority-bearing decisions as proof-carrying, attenuable capability leases spanning Skills, MCP tools, local execution, and subagents. R082 shows saved traces do not prove a residual advantage over the strongest provenance-authority or IFC/taint-style baselines; R083 shows the narrower lease-semantics delta is at least mechanically expressible and residual in a controlled suite.
- Claims safe to keep: proof-carrying leases as the policy object; structured intent certificates as the root of run-time authority; explicit influence modes tied to protected decision classes; deterministic checker outside the LLM TCB; temporal/budget/delegation attenuation; cross-extension enforcement and audit.
- Claims to narrow or drop: "first permission model for Skills/MCP" is too broad and conflicts with Progent, AgentBound, SkillGuard, and SkillScope. "First work to connect user intent to agent execution" conflicts with AuthGraph, Intent-to-Execution Integrity, and contextual-security papers. "First provenance-aware agent authorization" conflicts with AuthGraph, PACT, FIDES/RTBAS/NeuroTaint, and AIRGuard. "Solves prompt injection" must not be claimed. "Policy synthesis for agents" is too close to PCAS/Progent/AgentSpec unless tied specifically to proof-carrying, attenuable intent leases and decision-class influence constraints.
- Closest novelty risks: AuthGraph and PACT for clean user-intent authorization vs execution/argument provenance; ARGUS for influence-provenance decision auditing; AIRGuard and ClawGuard for authority-control/tool-boundary enforcement; Agent-Sentry for execution-provenance behavior bounding; CaMeL, Prompt Flow Integrity, FIDES, RTBAS, and NeuroTaint for control/data-flow and taint-style defenses; AgentSecBench and Formalizing LLM Agent Security for contextual/noninterference-style framing; Progent/PCAS/AgentSpec/AgentGuard/AgentGuardian/AgentBound for policy/access-control frameworks; VIGIL, SkillGuard, and SkillScope for Skill permissions/specs; SecureClaw/AgentSentry/ARM for runtime boundary/causal refinement; ActPlane for below-tool enforcement.
- Larger claim opportunity: portable intent-provenance-aware lease compiler for agent extension ecosystems. The paper should be about compiling and checking a richer authorization object for extension workflows, not about being the first to notice untrusted context can steer authority.
- Absorbable ideas to import: AuthGraph's clean authorization baseline, PACT's argument-role provenance, AIRGuard's authority-confusion taxonomy, AgentDojo oracles, CaMeL/PFI/FIDES explicit control/data split, RTBAS dependency screening, AgentSecBench-style projection/noninterference metrics, Task Shield's objective-serving check, Progent/PCAS/AgentSpec policy DSL comparisons, AgentBound/AgentGuard access-control baselines, SkillGuard/SkillScope Skill taxonomies, MCPTox/MCP DCI/WebMCP cases, tau2/tau3 domain policies, SecureClaw effect-sink/read-boundary split, ARM denial-feedback caveat, and ActPlane-style backend lowering.
